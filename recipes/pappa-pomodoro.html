Pappa al Pomodoro (Tomato and Bread Soup)

Description

Pappa al Pomodoro is a delicious and light soup made of tomato and bread, you can enjoy it hot or cold. It's a peasant-origin dish from Tuscany, used to recycle leftover bread and have a good and healthy meal.

Ingredients

  • Canned tomatoes (800 g)
  • Bread (300 g)
  • Water (to taste)
  • Garlic (1 clove)
  • Basil to taste
  • Extra virgin olive oil (to taste)
  • Fine salt (to taste)

Steps

  1. Heat gently some oil in a saucepan
  2. Peel the garlic and cut it into thin slices (if you don't like it, you can use it whole and unpeeled and remove it later) and fry lightly
  3. Pour in the tomato pulp and a bit of salt
  4. Let it cook gently for a generous half hour
  5. Meanwhile, cut the bread into slices and cut them in half
  6. When the sauce is ready, dilute it with some water and mix
  7. Immerse the bread slices, breaking them up if necessary so as to fit them all in the pot
  8. Press down lightly with the ladle
  9. Cover with a lid
  10. Let it rest for about 15 minutes
  11. Mix well and put it back on the stove and let it cook gently for another 5 minutes or until the desired consistency is reached: the bread slices must completely disintegrate and create a "pulp"
  12. Turn off the heat and adjust the salt, then finish with a generous drizzle of oil and a few basil leaves
  13. Mix well and serve hot, warm, or cold, garnishing the dish with an additional drizzle of oil
